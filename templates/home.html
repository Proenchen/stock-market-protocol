Welcome to the Global Stock Market project page!

Researchers have identified over 500 different characteristics that influence the stock market performance of firms. These anomalies often generate significant returns when used to construct a portfolio strategy. However, the reproducibility and future performance of these strategies still remain as open questions. While the initial results appear promising, there are ongoing concerns about whether these strategies can continue to perform well over time, especially in different market conditions. Many authors have proposed frameworks to address these issues, aiming to make the results more robust and reliable. These frameworks typically focus on improving the testing and validation processes to ensure that the strategies remain effective. Despite these advancements, the majority of studies and proposed frameworks have been centered around the U.S. market [1], which is well-known and widely studied. This focus on the U.S. market, however, overlooks the significant role played by other global markets, which can exhibit different dynamics and behaviors that might impact the performance of portfolio strategies. Therefore, there is a clear need for an open-access framework that enables researchers to analyze and test portfolio strategies across a wider range of global stock markets. Such a framework would provide valuable insights and allow researchers to better understand the performance of these strategies in different international contexts, ultimately enhancing the robustness of portfolio management research. This tool would contribute to the academic community by offering a more comprehensive view of portfolio strategy performance beyond just the U.S. market, supporting global research efforts in finance and investment strategy development.
